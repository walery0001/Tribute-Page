Roman Polanski

Film director, producer, writer, and actor

The most important dates and facts from the life of Roman Polanski

    Early life
      Roman Polanski born 18 August 1933 in Paris, he was the son of Bella Katz-Przedborska and Mojżesz Liebling.
      The Polański family moved back to the Polish city of Kraków in early 1937.His father was transferred, with thousands of other Jews. His mother was taken to Auschwitz, and was killed soon after arriving.
      Polański escaped the Kraków Ghetto in 1943. After the war, he was reunited with his father and moved back to Kraków.
    Early career
      He finished the National Film School in Łódź.
      Polanski's directorial debut was also in 1955 with a short film Rower (Bicycle).
    Marriages and relationships
      Polanski's first wife, Barbara Lass, was a Polish actress who also starred in Polanski's 1959 When Angels Fall. The couple were married in 1959 and divorced in 1961
      Polanski met rising actress Sharon Tate while filming The Fearless Vampire Killers, and during the production, the two of them began dating. On 20 January 1968, Polanski married Tate in London. In August 1969, while Polanski was in Europe working on a film, Tate was murdered along with four of their friends at their home in Los Angeles by members of Charles Manson's "family", a group of young, mostly female followers. Tate was pregnant at the time of her murder.
      In 1989, Polanski married his current wife, French actress Emmanuelle Seigner. They have two children, daughter Morgane and son Elvis
    2020s
      In February 2020, Polanski won Best Director at France's 2020 Cesar Awards.
